About Meal

Deep-fried chickpea fritters, often served in pita bread or with salads. They are a popular street food and can be found in many restaurants and food stalls throughout Egypt.

Ingridients:


- 1 cup dried chickpeas, soaked overnight and drained
- 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
- 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
- 1/4 cup chopped green onions
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
- 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1/8 teaspoon black pepper
- Vegetable oil for frying

Instruction:


- Combine ingredients: In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, cilantro, parsley, green onions, garlic, cumin, coriander, baking powder, salt, and pepper. Pulse until the mixture is finely chopped but not completely smooth.
- Form patties: Use your hands to shape the mixture into small patties or balls.
- Fry: Heat about 1 inch of vegetable oil in a deep skillet over medium heat. Carefully add the falafel patties to the hot oil and fry for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy.
- Drain: Remove the falafel patties from the oil and drain on paper towels.
- Serve: Serve the falafel hot or warm, in pita bread with your favorite toppings, or as part of a mezze platter.

Fattah

About Meal

A hearty dish made with layers of bread, rice, and meat or chicken, topped with a rich tomato sauce. It's a popular dish for special occasions and holidays.

Ingridients:


- For the Meat:
- 1 lb bone-in lamb or beef shoulder, cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 large onion, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Water to cover
- For the Rice:
- 1 cup long-grain rice
- 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
- Salt to taste
- For the Sauce:
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 onion, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 (14.5 ounce) can diced tomatoes, undrained
- 1 tablespoon tomato paste
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/4 teaspoon ground coriander
- Salt and pepper to taste
- For Serving:
- 4-6 pieces of pita bread, cut into small pieces
- Plain yogurt (optional)

Instruction:


- Make the Meat: In a large pot or Dutch oven, brown the meat on all sides over medium-high heat. Add the onion, garlic, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Pour in enough water to cover the meat. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 1-1.5 hours, or until the meat is very tender. Remove the meat from the pot and shred or chop it. Strain the broth and set aside.
- Cook the Rice: In a separate pot, bring the broth to a boil. Add the rice and salt. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
- Make the Sauce: Heat the olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the diced tomatoes, tomato paste, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.
- Assemble the Fattah: In a large serving bowl, layer the pita bread pieces, rice, shredded meat, and tomato sauce. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used.
- Serve: Serve the Fattah hot, garnished with yogurt if desired.

kousa mahshi

About Meal

Kousa Mahshi is a beloved Middle Eastern dish, particularly popular in Egypt. It involves hollowing out zucchini and stuffing them with a flavorful mixture of rice, meat (often ground beef or lamb), and spices. The stuffed zucchini are then simmered in a rich tomato sauce, creating a comforting and flavorful meal.

Ingridients:

Ingredients:
- For the Filling:
- 1 pound ground beef or lamb
- 1 cup long-grain rice
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley
- 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
- Salt and pepper to taste
- For the Sauce:
- 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
- 1 onion, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon tomato paste
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/4 teaspoon ground coriander
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Other Ingredients:
- 6-8 small zucchini
- 1 cup water
- Olive oil for frying

Instruction:


- Prepare the Filling:
- In a large skillet, brown the ground meat over medium heat.
- Add the chopped onion and garlic and cook until softened.
- Stir in the rice, parsley, mint, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few minutes more, until the rice is lightly toasted.
- Prepare the Zucchini:
- Trim the ends off the zucchini. Carefully scoop out the seeds using a spoon, being careful not to pierce the bottom.
- Stuff the Zucchini:
- Fill each zucchini cavity with the meat and rice mixture.
- Pack it in firmly but leave a little space at the top.
- Make the Sauce:
- In a separate pot, combine the crushed tomatoes, chopped onion, garlic, tomato paste, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
- Simmer the Zucchini:
- Arrange the stuffed zucchini in a large pot. Pour the tomato sauce over the zucchini, making sure they are mostly covered.
- Add 1 cup of water to the pot.
- Bring to a simmer over medium heat, then reduce heat to low, cover, and cook for 30-45 minutes, or until the zucchini are tender and the rice is cooked through.
- Serve:
- Serve the Kousa Mahshi hot, spooning some of the sauce over each zucchini.

About Meal

Hawawshi is a savory pie filled with spiced ground meat, onions, and peppers. It's a popular street food and can be found in many bakeries and food stalls throughout Egypt.

Ingridients:


- For the Filling:
- 1 pound ground beef or lamb
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 1 green bell pepper, finely chopped
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/4 teaspoon ground coriander
- 1/4 teaspoon paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste
- For the Dough:
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 cup warm water
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- For Baking:
- 1 egg, beaten

Instruction:


- Make the Filling:
- In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, onion, green bell pepper, cumin, coriander, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
- Make the Dough:
- In a separate bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add the warm water and olive oil, mixing until a soft dough forms. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
- Assemble the Hawawshi:
- Divide the dough into 8 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a ball.
- On a lightly floured surface, roll out each ball of dough into a circle about 6 inches in diameter.
- Place a spoonful of the meat mixture in the center of each circle.
- Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape. Pinch the edges together to seal.
- Place the Hawawshi on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
- Brush the tops of the Hawawshi with the beaten egg and sprinkle with sesame seeds (optional).
- Bake:
- Bake in a preheated oven at 400¬∞F (200¬∞C) for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown and the filling is cooked through.

It is characterized by its smoky flavor, smooth and creamy texture. Baba ghanoush is a popular cold appetizer and is usually served with Arabic bread or fresh vegetables.

Ingridients:


- Eggplant: It is the main ingredient in baba ghanoush and is roasted until it becomes black and soft.
- Garlic: Adds a strong and distinctive flavor to the dish.
- Lemon juice: Gives baba ghanoush a refreshing acidity.
- Olive oil: Adds a rich flavor and a creamy texture.
- Spices: Such as cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper.

Instruction:


- Roast the eggplant: Roast the eggplant over an open fire or in the oven until it becomes black and soft.
- Peel the eggplant: After roasting the eggplant, peel it and remove the seeds.
- Mash the eggplant: Mash the eggplant well until it becomes smooth.
- Add other ingredients: Add minced garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, and spices to the mashed eggplant and mix the ingredients well.
- Serving: Serve baba ghanoush cold with Arabic bread or fresh vegetables.
